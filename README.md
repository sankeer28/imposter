# Imposter Who?

A party deduction game for 3–20 players. One secret word. One liar at the table. Ask, answer, accuse — and figure out who doesn't belong.

---

## How to Play

1. **Gather your group** — pass the phone around the table, one player at a time.
2. **Each player holds to reveal their card** — everyone sees the secret word except the imposter, who sees `IMPOSTER` and a fuzzy hint clue.
3. **Go around the table** — each player says one word or phrase related to the secret word. The imposter must bluff.
4. **Discuss** — talk, question each other, look for the person whose clue felt slightly off.
5. **Vote** — agree on who you think the imposter is.
6. **Reveal** — tap **Reveal Imposter & Word** to see if you were right.

The imposter wins if they survive the vote without being identified. Everyone else wins if they correctly identify the imposter.

## Features

### 🤖 Four-Tier AI Word Engine

Every round, a fresh word + hint is generated by AI — no stale word lists. The client tries each server in sequence and shows live status if one fails:

| Tier | Model | Limit |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Gemini 2.0 Flash | 1,500 req/day (free) |
| 2 | Groq qwen3-32b (thinking) | 14,400 req/day (free) |
| 3 | OpenRouter gpt-oss-120b:free | Free tier |
| 4 | Ollama Cloud gemma3:4b | Free tier |
| 5 | Hardcoded list (~490 words) | Unlimited offline fallback |

If Server 1 fails the app immediately tries Server 2 and shows `"Server 1 failed, trying Server 2…"` so you never just stare at a spinner. You only hit the offline fallback if all four AI servers are down simultaneously.

### 🧠 The Two-Test Hint Rule

This is what separates **Imposter Who?** from every other word-guessing app. The hint given to the imposter must pass **two strict tests**:

**Test 1 — Widely Known:** most people would immediately nod and say *"yeah, that's true of it."* The hint can't be obscure trivia.

> ❌ `cucumber → ribbed` — most people don't describe cucumbers as ribbed. The imposter sounds clueless.
> ❌ `shark → cartilage` — biology class knowledge, not something you'd naturally say.

**Test 2 — Not The First Thing:** it can't be the single most iconic or defining property. If the hint gives away the word immediately, there's no game.

> ❌ `clock → ticking` — that's *the* clock thing. Too obvious.
> ❌ `pen → ink` — first word anyone says about a pen.
> ❌ `banana → yellow` — that just *is* banana.

**Both tests must pass.** The hint must be widely known *and* not too defining:

> ✅ `cashew → curved` — everyone knows the shape, but "curved" fits hundreds of things.
> ✅ `clock → round` — obviously true, but round fits clocks, coins, plates, planets.
> ✅ `candle → drips` — everyone's seen wax drip, but "drips" doesn't scream candle.
> ✅ `guitar → hollow` — widely known, but hollow also fits drums, caves, chocolate eggs.
> ✅ `shark → silent` — people know sharks are quiet hunters, but silent fits many things.

The hint also must be a **direct physical or behavioural property** of the thing itself — not a cultural association, movie reference, song, or brand:

> ❌ `Ghost → music` — that's a movie association, not a property of ghosts.
> ❌ `piano → lacquered` — too specific, sounds weird in conversation.

This means the imposter can drop the hint word naturally into discussion and sound believable — without the hint being a dead giveaway for everyone else.

### 🔄 Fair Imposter Rotation

Nobody gets stuck as the imposter every round. The game tracks who has been imposter and rotates through all players before repeating anyone. Fresh players who haven't been imposter yet are always picked first. The cycle resets once everyone has had a turn.

### 🧩 Per-Player Word History

The game remembers which words each player has already seen — even across sessions. Before fetching a new word, it builds the union of all current players' seen words and sends that list to the AI with instructions to avoid them. So even if your group plays dozens of rounds, you won't see the same word twice.

This works across partial group changes too — if three out of five players have seen "jungle," the AI still avoids it.
